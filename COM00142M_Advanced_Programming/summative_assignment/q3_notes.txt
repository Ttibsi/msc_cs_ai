"Automated social media moderation systems trained on historically biased reporting data inevitably perpetuate and amplify platform discrimination against certain topics and user groups."
800 words

Structure:
Intro (100 words)
Arguement 1 (200 words) - ethics (community beliefs)
    * It's not OK to share certain things publicly (violence, gore, extreme pornography)
    * age restrictions are often 13 or 16, teenagers still developing
        - mention the age restrictions going through law right now
Arguement 2 (200 words) - morals (personal beliefs)
Arguement 3 (200 words) - legal
    * platforms are responsible for what's uploaded
Conclusion (100 words)

Notes
* pro stance 
* counter-arguemnt every point and refute it
* responsibilities of software engineers here

Argument 1 - foreign languages/other cultures are less well represented in the training data
    - counter arguement: Millions use social media in non-western world countries too
        - rebuttal: Largest social media companies are US-centric, with China having it's own social media behind the great firewall
    - examples: https://www.oversightboard.com/decision/ig-7thr3si1/

            https://worldpopulationreview.com/country-rankings/social-media-users-by-country

Argument 2 - context-less moderation has led to higher amounts of self-censorship online
    Counter argument: This isn't a recent development, see leetspeak or slang.
        rebuttal: This is more than slang, educators are affected, this is stretching into the real world
