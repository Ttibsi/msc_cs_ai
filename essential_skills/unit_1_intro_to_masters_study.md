### Lesson 1 - Introduction
Bloom's Taxonomy - a framework for higher thinking
This is a pyramid of layers. From bottom to top:
    - Remember
    - Understand
    - Apply
    - Analyzse
    - Evaluate
    - Create

Used in designing and marking assignments
Masters level study is about using your knowledge to achieve the top 3: 
    analysing, evaluating, creating

    * how and why something works
    * reasons and implications
    * comparing and contrasting
    * justifying choices
    * judging something's value (most effective solution)

Critical thinking and analysis / higher level thinking
* questioning accepted thinking
* analysing against evidence
* making a decision based on logical, systematic, and rigorous thinking

Depth of knowledge - does not mean more subjects, means understanding in more detail
 - know, understand, and use advanced theories and methodologies

Theory application - understand and apply a range of theories
    - develop an optimal solution for a given problem

* Be prepared to present your own ideas and/or work

### Lesson 2 - Independant Learning
* Can study/work without direct direction
* Take responsibility for workload
* make decisions about what you will focus on
* active learning - engaging with the material

- set goals, plan studying, actively seek and reflect on feedback, communicate with others

Time management - allocate time for
* studying
* research
* practice
* other commitments and responsibilities

### Lesson 3 - Academic reading and writing
Academic reading:
    * critical analysis
    * synthesising, and questioning material to draw well-informed conclusions

Academic writing:
* supports the development of critical thinking
* helps to develop arguments and present data/research
* Objective and impartial
    - any POV should be explicit and acknowledged
* appropriate use of terminology
* adherence to academic conventions
    - such as referencing standards
* coherence and flow

### Lesson 4 - Academic expectations
Five principals of academic integrity:
- Honesty - acknowledge your own work and what contributions came from others
- Trust - others can trust your findings are your own
- Fairness - show good academic practice and treat other students doing the same fairly
- Respect - respect diverse opinions and ideas across the academic community
- Responsibility - understanding academic integrity and applying it to your work

Avoiding plagarism
- cite sources properly
- paraphrase and summarise
- use quotations
- manage references properly

Generative AI and academic practices
* can be used with drafting text, summarising articles, generating code, producing synthetic data
* Can't replace critical thinking, independant research, deep subject knowledge
* use of GenAI should be listed in a footnote or appendix
