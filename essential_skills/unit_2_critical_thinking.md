### Lesson 1 - Critical Thinking
- Analysis - Breaking down info into it's constituent parts to understand it's meaning
- Evaluation - Assessing the credibility, accuracy, and validity of information and arguments
- Interpretation - Understanding the significance and implications of information and ideas
- Inferrence - Drawing logical conclusions and making reasoned judgements based on evidence
- Explanation - Clearly and coherently articulating reasoning and conclusions
- Self-regulation - reflecting and monitoring ones own biases and assumptions

critical thinking for problem solving
    - gather relevant info
    - evaluate potential solutions
    - making informed decisions based on evidence and logical reasoning

Critical - being thoughtful, asking questions about what you are reading, don't take anything
at face value.
    - ask questions about the strenghs and weaknesses of an argument or idea
    - analyse and evaluate informatuion/theories/practices
    - be aware of yours and others biases

Blooms Taxonomy
- Knowledge
- Understanding
    - "what facts or ideas show...", "which statements support..."
- Application
    - "what would result if...", "what facts would you select to show..."
- Analysis
    - draw connections between ideas and concepts
    - Answer questions like "why", "how", "what if"
- Synthesis
    - Use given facts/connections to create new theories
    - synthesise information in a different way
    - "what changes would you make to solve...", "how would you improve..."
- Evaluation
    - Judge, assess, criticise information
    - Make a decision to support your work
        - Produce new or original work
    - "So what...", "What's next..."

Try to avoid confirmation bias

Critically thinking about a text
1 - get a description
    - the 5 Ws
2 - Analysis
    - How, Why, What if 
    - EX: How was the research conducted, why are these theories discussed
    - Does the data presented actually support their conclusion
3 - Evaluation
    - " So What", "Whats next"
    - "How relevant is this text, what is your position, how does this relate to other information"
    - Evaluate types of evidence
        - empirical, statistical, expert testimony, qualitative data, historical records, 
        - case studies, theoretical models

#### Lesson 1 exercise
https://www.aarp.org/health/healthy-living/organic-food/
Summarise the article:
* Article is trying to convince you that organic is good, but eating local food is better
* Very casual tone

Clarity and Logic - badly laid out and unclear as to exactly what it's trying to
                    convince us of. Potentially unfounded claims.
Evidence - No sources, very little references to actual data or research.
         - Seems to be only picking facts that support their argument
         - Some claims in this seem unfounded because of the lack of evidence
            - and unlikely to be true
Bias and Perspective - Author seems to be bias, only stating their own argument
                        without addressing alternatives

Contradictions and Leaps - Author appears to say that local food has better 
                           quality by default, with no evidence or reason to
                           suspect this. They also talk about "healthy,nutritious meat", 
                           then say that the reader should have a "plant-predominant
                           diet" to dismiss significant amounts of meat eating.
